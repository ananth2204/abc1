 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   INTRODUCTION:                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   TSUPDATE IS A UTILITY PROGRAM WRITTEN TO PROVIDE MASS CHANGE CAPABILITY                                                        *
 *   OF PARTITIONED DATA SETS OR SEQUENTIAL FILES.  TSUPDATE CAN PROVIDE A                                                          *
 *   BATCH USER MOST OF SPF/EDIT'S FUNCTION PLUS MANY THAT ARE CURRENTLY NOT                                                        *
 *   AVAILABLE ANYWHERE.                                                                                                            *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   PROCESSING NOTE:                                                                                                               *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *     SOME USERS HAVE ASKED WHY SO MANY OPTIONS ON THE PARM FIELD AND OTHERS ON                                                    *
 *   THE CONTROL CARDS, THE REASONS ARE: ALL PARM FIELD OPTIONS ARE IN EFFECT FOR                                                   *
 *   THE DURATION OF THE TSUPDATE RUN WHERE THOSE ON THE CONTROL CARDS ARE USED                                                     *
 *   ONLY FOR THE EXECUTION OF THAT CONTROL CARD. DEPENDING ON WHAT OPTIONS AND                                                     *
 *   WHAT COMBINATION OF OPTIONS THE USER SELECTS CAN INCREASE TSUPDATE PROCESSING                                                  *
 *   TIME CONSIDERABLY, BUT AT THE SAME TIME INCREASING THE ACCURACY OF THE AMOUNT                                                  *
 *   OF CHANGES. WE LEAVE IT UP TO THE USER TO DESCRIBE HIS DATA PROCESSING NEEDS                                                   *
 *   AND TRY TO MAKE TSUPDATE AS FLEXIBLE AS POSSIBLE TO MEET THOSE REQUIREMENTS.                                                   *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   PARTITIONED DATA SET DESCRIPTION OF FUNCTION.                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   FUNCTION:  READ/WRITE LEVEL.                                                                                                   *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   TSUPDATE READS AND WRITES AT TRACK LEVEL RATHER THAN DATA SET LEVEL,                                                           *
 *   HENCE ANY UPDATES APPLIED BY TSUPDATE ARE DONE IN PLACE.  THEREFORE, NO                                                        *
 *   MORE SPACE IS USED IN THE PDS AND FREQUENT COMPRESSES ARE NOT                                                                  *
 *   REQUIRED.                                                                                                                      *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   PROCESSING NOTE:(R E V I S I O N  6.4) A WORD ABOUT R.A.C.F. OR SIMULAR SECURITY SYSTEMS.                                      *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *    WE HAVE BEEN REQUESTED BY USERS THAT TSUPDATE "KNOWS' WHETHER IT IS GOING TO WRITE OR NOT                                     *
 *    BASED ON THE WRITE OPTION OF THE CONTROL CARD. WE SHOULD OPEN THE PDS ACCORDING TO THIS                                       *
 *    OPTION(INPUT OR UPDATE). THIS WOULD ALLOW TSUPDATE RUNS AGAINST RACF PROTECTED DATASETS WITH                                  *
 *    UNIVERSIAL ACCESS OF READ. HOWEVER THIS REQUIRES US  TO OPEN THE PROCLIB DD CARD AND CLOSE IT                                 *
 *    FOR EVERY INPUT CONTROL CARD.IN PREVIOUS RELEASES WE BELIEVED THE PERFORMANCE IMPACT TO GREAT                                 *
 *    TO IMPLEMENT THIS REQUEST.THIS RELEASE HAS IMPLEMENTED BETTER I/O OVERLAP,THAT WE NOW CAN                                     *
 *    AFFORD THE "OVERHEAD" OF ALL THE OPENS AND CLOSES. REVISION 6.4 WILL NOW "HONOR" YOUR WRITE                                   *
 *    OPTION FOR THESE SECURITY SYSTEMS ,HOWEVER YOU STILL WILL REQUIRE READ AUTHORITY TO THE PDS.                                  *
 *            AS A STATEMENT OF POLICY TSUPDATE IN NO WAY ALTERS SYSTEM  CONTROL BLOCKS NOR WILL IT                                 *
 *    ABRIDGE THE FUNCTIONS OF COMPUTER SECURITY SYSTEMS.                                                                           *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   PROCESSING NOTE:                                                                                                               *
 *              VARIABLE BLOCKSIZE INPUT(REVISION#5.0 3350 SUPPORT)                                                                 *
 *              VARIABLE BLOCKSIZE INPUT(REVISION#6.0 3380 SUPPORT)                                                                 *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   TSUPDATE WHEN PROCESSING A PDS, HAS A MAXIMUM BLOCK SIZE LIMITATION OF                                                         *
 *   13K FOR A 3330-1  ,  19K FOR A 3350 AND 32K OF A 3380.                                                                         *
 *   ANY PDS WITH A LARGER BLOCK SIZE WILL CAUSE A TSUPDATE USER ABEND.                                                             *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   SEQUENTIAL FILE PROCESSING CAN HANDLE A BLOCK SIZE OF 32K AND THERE IS                                                         *
 *   NO RESTRICTION ON THE INPUT OR OUTPUT FILE BLOCK SIZES.                                                                        *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   TSUPDATE DOES NOT SUPPORT RECFM=V IN ANY FORM.                                                                                 *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   FUNCTION:  DATA MANIPULATION                                                                                                   *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   TSUPDATE HAS THE ABILITY TO SEARCH AND REPLACE ARGUMENTS OF DIFFERENT                                                          *
 *   LENGTHS, THEREBY GIVING THE USER THE CAPABILITY TO INSERT DATA OR                                                              *
 *   DELETE DATA IN A RECORD.                                                                                                       *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   ANY OF THE 256 CHARACTERS IN THE IBM CHARACTER SET MAY BE SEARCHED ON OR                                                       *
 *   RELACED, EXCEPT THE POUND SIGN(#),WHICH IS USED AS THE DELIMITER FOR THE                                                       *
 *   CONTROL CARD.                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   FUNCTION:  DOUBLE SEARCH CRITERIA                                                                                              *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   THIS IS A NEW FEATURE TO TSUPDATE NOT FOR PDS PROCESSING BUT, SEQUENTIAL                                                       *
 *   PROCESSING THAT NOW HAS THE ABILITY TO UTILIZE THE DOUBLE SEARCH FUNCTION.                                                     *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   THIS FUNCTION SUPPORTED BY BOTH PDS AND SEQUENTIAL PROCESSING ALLOWS                                                           *
 *   THE USER TO SET TWO (2) SEARCH ARGUMENTS PER CONTROL CARD.  BOTH SEARCH                                                        *
 *   ARGUMENTS MUST BE FOUND IN THE DATA RECORD BEFORE THE DATA IS CHANGED.                                                         *
 *   THE FIRST ARGUMENT ON THE SEARCH CARD, COL 1, IS THE PRIMARY SEARCH.                                                           *
 *   STARTING IN COL #1 OF THE INPUT RECORD ALL 80 COLUMNS ARE CHECKED FOR                                                          *
 *   THAT STRING OF DATA, AND IF FOUND, THE SECOND SEARCH ARGUMENT IS                                                               *
 *   SEARCHED FOR IN THE INPUT RECORD AGAIN STARTING OVER FROM COL 1.  IF                                                           *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   THE SECOND SEARCH ARGUMENT IS FOUND, IT IS CHANGED BY THE REPLACE                                                              *
 *   ARGUMENT.                                                                                                                      *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   AT THIS POINT, THE SECOND ARGUMENT AND THE REPLACE ARGUMENTS ARE                                                               *
 *   TREATED AS IF DOUBLE PROCESSING WAS NOT IN EFFECT AND TSUPDATE WILL                                                            *
 *   PROCESS THE INPUT RECORD AS UNDER VARIABLE LENGTH CHANGES.                                                                     *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   FUNCTION:  COMPELTE DELETION OF SEARCH ARGUMENT.                                                                               *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   THIS REVISION LEVEL OF TSUPDATE PROVIDES THE USER WITH THE ABILITY TO                                                          *
 *   DELETE A SEARCH ARGUMENT BY PROVIDING TSUPDATE A NULL REPLACE ARGUMENT.                                                        *
 *   THIS FUNCTION IS SUPPORTED BY THE SEQUENTIAL AND PDS PROCESSORS, AND                                                           *
 *   ALSO FOR DOUBLE AND SINGLE SEARCH FUNCTION.                                                                                    *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *        EX   COL.1 OF CONTROL CARD                                                                                                *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *             #SYSDA##                                                                                                             *
 *              *     *--> NULL REPLACE ARGUMENT                                                                                    *
 *              V                                                                                                                   *
 *              SEARCH ARGUMENT                                                                                                     *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   ALL OUTPUT ON THE CHANGE LISTING FOR THE DELETE OPTION WILL NOW                                                                *
 *   REFLECT AFTER THE WORD "REPLACEMENT" THE TEXT "WITH SEARCH DELETED".                                                           *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   FUNCTION:  CONTROL AND SUMMARY LOG PRINTOUT.                                                                                   *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   TSUPDATE PROVIDES THE USER WITH A LOG PAGE LISTNG OF CONTROL                                                                   *
 *   INFORMATION, PROCESSING OPTIONS, ERROR MESSAGES, IF ANY, AND THE RESULT                                                        *
 *   OF THE PROCESSING INCLUDING RECORD COUNTS, RECORD CHANGE COUNTS, AND                                                           *
 *   RECORDS IN ERROR, AND NOW GENERATE COUNTS.                                                                                     *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   FUNCTION:  CHANGED DATA LISTING                                                                                                *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   TSUPDATE SEPARATES THE OUTPUT FROM EACH CONTROL CARD WITH BIG BLOCK AND                                                        *
 *   PROVIDES A RUN NUMBER WHICH ALSO WILL BE REFLECTED ON THE SUMMARY LOG                                                          *
 *   UNDER THAT CONTROL CARD PRINTED OUTPUT.                                                                                        *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   FUNCTION:  ABNORMAL FAILURE RECOVERY.                                                                                          *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   THIS REVISION LEVEL OF TSUPDATE PROVIDES THE USER WITH RECOVERY FROM                                                           *
 *   I/O ERRORS, TSUPDATE CODE FAILURES, SYSTEM ABENDS (B37,D37,E37, ETC.)                                                          *
 *   BUT NOT TSUPDATE USER ABENDS.                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   TSUPDATE WILL TRAP ALL ABEND CONDITIONS AND DETERMINE IF THEY ARE                                                              *
 *   RECOVERABLE, AND IF SO WILL STOP ALL PROCESSING OF THE CURRENT CONTROL                                                         *
 *   CARD, PROVIDE MESSAGES ON THE CONTROL LOG AS TO THE FAILURE TYPE AND                                                           *
 *   THE PROGRAM IN TSUPDATE WHO FAILED, AND WILL TRY TO CONTINUE                                                                   *
 *   PROCESSING WITH THE NEXT CONTROL CARD, THE ONLY TIME TSUPDATE WILL                                                             *
 *   PROVIDE A "DUMP" IS FROM NON-RECOVERABLE SITUATIONS.                                                                           *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   IF THE FAILURES AS NOTED ON THE CONTROL LOG APPEAR TO BE CODING                                                                *
 *   PROBLEMS IN TSUPDATE, PLEASE NOTIFY TECHNICAL SERVICES.                                                                        *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   THE ADDRESS WILL PRINT OUT ON THE LOG AS TO WHERE TO SEND THE                                                                  *
 *   DOCUMENTATION TO CONRAIL.PLEASE USE "USER FEEDBACK FORM " AT THE                                                               *
 *   END OF THIS DOCUMENTATION.                                                                                                     *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   FUNCTION: PARM FIELD OPTIONS PRINT ON LOG PAGE                                                                                 *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *    DUE TO THE NUMBER OF OPTIONS AVAILABLE AND TO BETTER DOCUMENT WHAT WAS                                                        *
 *   SELECTED BY THE USER WHEN PROBLEMS ARE SENT TO TECHNICAL SERVICES, WE HAVE                                                     *
 *   ADDED IMMEDIATELY AFTER THE DISCLAIMER ON THE LOG PAGE A PRINTOUT OF ALL                                                       *
 *   OPTIONS SELECTED AND/OR DEFAULTED FOR THAT RUN.                                                                                *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   TSUPDATE PARM FIELD PROCESSING OPTIONS:                                                                                        *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   A PARM FIELD MUST BE SUPPLIED BY THE USER FOR TSUPDATE TO FUNCTION AND                                                         *
 *   IT MUST BE IN THE FOLLOWING FORMAT:                                                                                            *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   PARM FIELD PROCESSING OPTIONS:                                                                                                 *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   FIELD ONE(1):                                                                                                                  *
 *   PARM='XREFFUL PRODUCE FULL PROCLIB CROSS REFERENCE                                                                             *
 *   PARM='XREFDSN PRODUCE DATASET PROCLIB CROSS REFERENCE                                                                          *
 *   PARM='XREFPGM PRODUCE PROGRAM PROCLIB CROSS REFERENCE                                                                          *
 *   PARM='PDS     PROCESS A PDS                                                                                                    *
 *   PARM='SEQ     PROCESS A SEQ DATA SET                                                                                           *
 *   PARM=',       DEFAULT- PROCESS A PDS                                                                                           *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
 *   FUNCTION: PROCLIB CROSS REFERENCE FACILITY                                                                                     *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *    THIS FUNCTION WILL PROVIDE A CROSS REFERENCE LISTING OF A PROCLIB.                                                            *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *    OPTION XREFFUL WILL PRODUCE A CROSS REFERENCE LISTING BY PROC                                                                 *
 *    MEMBER , STEP NUMBER , STEP NAME , PROGRAM , DD NAME , DATASET.                                                               *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *    OPTION XREFDSN WILL PRODUCE A CROSS REFERENCE LISTING BY DATASET ,                                                            *
 *    PROC NAME , STEP NUMBER ,STEP NAME , PROGRAM , DD NAME.                                                                       *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *    OPTION XREFPGM WILL PRODUCE A CROSS REFERENCE LISTING BY PROGRAM ,                                                            *
 *    PROC NAME , STEP NUMBER ,STEP NAME , DD NAME , DATASET.                                                                       *
 *                                                                                                                                  *

 *                                                                                                                                  *

 *   DOUBLE SEARCH CRITERIA AND THE "REPLACE" ARGUMENT WAS LARGER THAN THE SEARCH                                                   *
 *   ARGUMENT, TSUPDATE FOLLOWING THE RULES JCL SYNTAX WOULD CALCULATE THE NUMBER                                                   *

 *   OR EQUAL TO THAT REQUIRED, TSUPDATE WOULD SLIDE THE JCL AROUND ON "THAT"                                                       *

 *   TSUPDATE WOULD PRINT THE CARD IT WAS TRYING TO UPDATE WITH THE MESSAGE                                                         *
 *   "INSUFFICENT ROOM TO UPDATE-DO MANUALLY". IT WOULD ALSO PUT A WARNING MESSAGE                                                  *
 *   FOR THAT CONTROL CARD ON THE LOG PAGE THAT "ONE OR MORE CARDS COULD NOT BE                                                     *
 *   UPDATED" AND WOULD REFLECT IN THE CONTROL TOTALS THE ACTUAL NUMBER OF UPDATE                                                   *

 *   KEYWORD (UNIT=SYSDA,) THAT COULD BE MOVED FROM THE CARD TO BE UPDATED THAT                                                     *

 *   DATASET BEING PROCESSED AND ALLOCATE A NEW DATASET DURING EXECUTION.                                                           *
 *   THIS OPTION CIRCUMVENTS THE PROBLEM OF RERUNNING TSUPDATE FOR EVERY                                                            *
 *   DIFFERING DATASET THAT REQUIRES PROCESSING. THE FOLLOWING PAGES GO                                                             *
 *   ON TO DESCRIBE EACH FUNCTION AND GIVE AN EXAMPLE OF EACH.                                                                      *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *              DYNAMIC FUNCTION CHANGE(CONTINUED)                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *          CONTROL CARD SYNTAX :                                                                                                   *
 *   CARD                                                                                                                           *
 *   COL1..........................................                                                                                 *
 *      INIT OPTION=SET,KEYWORD=********,VALUE=*********                                                                            *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *        THIS CONTROL CARD IS THE INTERFACE BETWEEN THE USER AND THE CONTROL                                                       *
 *   PROCESSORS AND MUST BE CODED CORRECTLY. THE 'OPTION=' PARAMETER IN THE                                                         *
 *   ABOVE DISPLAYED CONTROL CARD MUST SPECIFY 'SET' IN ORDER FOR TSUPDATE
 *   TO PERFORM DYNAMIC CHANGES. 'OPTION=SET' ALLOWS THE PROGRAM TO PASS                                                            *
 *   CONTROL TO THE 'INITSET' CSECT.  INITSET IS THE CONTROL SECTION
 *   WITHIN TSUPDATE WHICH ANCHORS ALL DYNAMIC FUNCTIONS.                                                                           *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *        THE 'KEYWORD=' PARAMETER DETAILS TO INITSET WHICH FUNCTION THE                                                            *
 *   USER WISHES TO DYNAMICALLY CHANGE.                                                                                             *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *        THE 'VALUE=' PARAMETER PASSES ADDITIONAL INFORMATION AS TO WHAT                                                           *
 *   TO DO WITH THE FUNCTION IMPLIED IN THE KEYWORD PARAMETER. THE FOLLOWING                                                        *
 *   LISTS THE COMBINATIONS AVAILABLE FOR PROCESSING UNDER THE INITIALIZATION                                                       *
 *   STATEMENT.                                                                                                                     *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *              DYNAMIC FUNCTION CHANGE(CONTINUED)                                                                                  *
 *   FUNCTION: DYNAMIC DETAIL PROCESSING                                                                                            *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   PARAMETERS:                                                                                                                    *
 *                KEYWORD=DETAIL,VALUE=ON                                                                                           *
 *                                     OFF                                                                                          *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *          DETAIL-ON/OFF  ALLOWS USERS TO OVERRIDE TSUPDATE PARAMETERS                                                             *
 *          ISSUED BY PROGRAM PARAMETERS WHEN TSUPDATE WAS SUBMITTED OR                                                             *
 *          WITH A PREVIOUSLY EXECUTED INITIALIZATION CONTROL CARD.                                                                 *
 *          THIS OPTION PERMITS THE USER TO SELECTIVELY APPLY THE DETAIL FUNCTIONS
 *          ON ONLY THOSE CONTROL CARDS HE DEEMS NECESSARY AND AVOIDS
 *          THE PROBLEM OF DETAILING ALL CONTROL CARDS FOR THAT RUN.
 *          THIS IN TURN RELIEVES THE LARGE QUANTITIES OF PAPER PRODUCED
 *          IN A RUN OF THIS NATURE.
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   OPTION (1): DETAIL-ON SUPPLIES THE USER WITH A LISTING OF CHANGES THAT                                                         *
 *               INCLUDE ANY EXEC OR PROC STATEMENTS ENCOUNTERED DURING PROCESSING.                                                 *
 *   OPTION (2): DETAIL-OFF LISTS ONLY THE CHANGES THAT WERE APPLIED TO                                                             *
 *               THE PDS MEMBERS AND NOT THE EXEC OR PROC CARDS ASSOCIATED                                                          *
 *               WITH THEM.                                                                                                         *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *              DYNAMIC FUNCTION CHANGE(CONTINUED)                                                                                  *
 *   FUNCTION: DYNAMIC CHECKPOINT PROCESSING                                                                                        *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   PARAMETERS:                                                                                                                    *
 *                KEYWORD=CKPT,VALUE=FULL                                                                                           *
 *                                   SHRT                                                                                           *
 *                                   NULL                                                                                           *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *         USE OF THE DYNAMIC CHECKPOINT FUNCTION ALLOW USERS TO DYNAMICALLY                                                        *
 *         CHANGE THEIR CHECKPOINT STATUS ACCORDING TO THE PARAMETER USED                                                           *
 *         WITH THE VALUE=.                                                                                                         *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   OPTION (1): CKPT-FULL WILL TAKE A CHECKPOINT AFTER EVERY 50 BLOCKS OF                                                          *
 *               DATA ON FILE TSUPDATE IS PROCESSING AGAINST.*                                                                      *
 *   OPTION (2): CKPT-SHRT WILL TAKE A CHECKPOINT BEFORRE PROCESSING EACH                                                           *
 *               CONTROL CARD. WITH THIS OPTION A USER COULD REPROCESS                                                              *
 *               AT ANY POINT AS LONG AS THERE WAS MULITPLE CONTROL CARD                                                            *
 *               PROCESSING.*                                                                                                       *
 *   OPTION (3): CKPT-NULL WILL TURN OFF ANY CHECKPOINT PROCESSING THAT WAS                                                         *
 *               INITIATED EITHER THROUGH TSUPDATE PARAMETERS OR PREVIOUS                                                           *
 *               USE OF THE INITIALIZATION CONTROL CARD.                                                                            *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   * PROCESSING NOTE                                                                                                              *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   WHEN USING THE DYNAMIC CHECKPOINT OPTION AS WHEN SUPPLIED THROUGH                                                              *
 *   THE PROGRAM PARAMETERS, A CHKPT DD STATEMENT MUST BE INCLUDED IN                                                               *
 *   THE SUBMIT JCL.                                                                                                                *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *              DYNAMIC FUNCTION CHANGE(CONTINUED)                                                                                  *
 *   FUNCTION: DYNAMIC NUMBER PROCESSING                                                                                            *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   PARAMETERS:                                                                                                                    *
 *                KEYWORD=NUM,VALUE=ON                                                                                              *
 *                                  OFF                                                                                             *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   OPTION (1): NUM-ON WILL CAUSE THE PROCESSING OF ONLY THE FIRST 71 COLUMNS                                                      *
 *               OF THE INPUT CARD AS DATA AND WILL ASSUME COL. 72 AS CONTINUATION                                                  *
 *               AND COLS. 73-80 AS RESERVED FOR NUMBERING PURPOSES.                                                                *
 *   OPTION (2): NUM-OFF WILL RESULT IN THE PROCESSING OF ALL 80 COLUMNS OF                                                         *
 *               THE INPUT CARD AS DATA. THIS OPTION SHOULD NOT BE USED TO                                                          *
 *               PROCESS JCL.                                                                                                       *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *              DYNAMIC FUNCTION CHANGE(CONTINUED)                                                                                  *
 *   FUNCTION: DYNAMIC LOOKBACK PROCESSING                                                                                          *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   PARAMETERS:                                                                                                                    *
 *                KEYWORD=LOOKBACK,VALUE=ON                                                                                         *
 *                                       OFF                                                                                        *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   OPTION (1): LOOKBACK-ON ALLOWS USER TO SEARCH FOR AN ARGUMENT AND                                                              *
 *               UPDATE IT IF AND ONLY IF A PREVIOUS SEARCH ARGUMENT WAS                                                            *
 *               ALSO FOUND. THAT IS, PROCESSING SEARCHES FOR A PRIMARY                                                             *
 *               SEARCH ARGUMENT AND IF IT IS FOUND, LOOKS AT THE PRECEDING                                                         *
 *               CODE ASSOCIATED WITH THE SAME DD FOR A SECOUND SEARCH ARGUMENT.                                                    *
 *               IF THIS SECOND ARGUMENT IS ALSO FOUND, THE CHANGES ARE THEN MADE.*                                                 *
 *   OPTION (2): LOOKBACK-OFF ELIMINATES ALL LOOKBACK PROCEDURES INCORPORATED                                                       *
 *               EITHER THROUGH THE PROGRAM PARAMETERS OR A PREVIOUS USE OF THE                                                     *
 *               DYNAMIC FUNCTION CHANGING INITIALIZATION CONTROL CARD.                                                             *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   * PROCESSING NOTE                                                                                                              *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *        USE OF THIS OPTION MUST BE ACCOMPANIED WITH A 'D' IN COLUMN 71 OF                                                         *
 *        THE CONTROL CARD WHICH DEFINES THE CHANGES. THIS WILL ALERT THE PROGRAM                                                   *
 *        THAT A DOUBLE SEARCH SHOULD BE INITIATED.                                                                                 *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 ************************************************************************************************************************************
1************************************************************************************************************************************
 *              DYNAMIC FUNCTION CHANGE(CONTINUED)                                                                                  *
 *   NEW FUNCTION: MULTI-PDS PROCESSING                                                                                             *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *               THE FINAL FUNCTION INTRODUCED BY TSUPDATE VERSION 6.5 IS THE                                                       *
 *               ABILITY TO PROCESS MORE THAN ONE PDS IN A SINGLE RUN OF TSUPDATE.                                                  *
 *               USING THE SAME INITIALIZATION CONTROL CARD INTRODUCED ABOVE,                                                       *
 *               A USER CAN COMMAND THE PROGRAM TO CLOSE AND DYNAMICALLY UNALLOCATE                                                 *
 *               A PROCESSING PDS AND IN TURN DYNAMICALLY ALLOCATE A NEW PDS TO BE                                                  *
 *               PROCESSED. ALL OF THIS OCCURS WHILE THE TSUPDATE PROGRAM IS STILL                                                  *
 *               IN EXECUTION.                                                                                                      *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   SYNTAX:                                                                                                                        *
 *               CARD                                                                                                               *
 *               COL1...........................................................                                                    *
 *                  INIT OPTION=SET,KEYWORD=PROCLIB,VALUE=***************                                                           *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *               IN THE CONTROL CARD:                                                                                               *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *               OPTION -  MUST EQUAL SET. THIS PASSES CONTROL TO THE INITSET CSECT                                                 *
 *                         WHICH CONTROLS THIS OPTION                                                                               *
 *               KEYWORD - MUST CONTAIN THE DDNAME OF THE DD STATEMENT WHICH CONTAINS                                               *
 *                         THE DATASET NAME OF THE PDS CURRENTLY BEING PROCESSED                                                    *
 *               VALUE -   MUST CONTAIN THE NAME OF THE NEW DATASET THAT THE USER                                                   *
 *                         WISHES TO PROCESS. THIS PARAMETER CONFORMS TO REGULAR DSN                                                *
 *                         CONVENTIONS IN LENGTH AND CONTENTS.                                                                      *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *

 *              CONTROL CARD STATISTICS                                                                                             *
 *   NEW FUNCTION:   REVISION 6.6.1       08/07/85                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                          TSUPDATE PERFORMANCE STATISTICS.                                                                        *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *     ADDITIONAL STATISTICS WILL NOW BE GENERATED WITH THIS VERSION OF                                                             *
 *   TSUPDATE.  THESE NEW STATISTICS WILL GIVE THE CPU TIME, SRB TIME, AND                                                          *
 *   ELAPSED TIME FOR EACH CONTROL CARD THAT IS PROCESSED, AND FOR THE TOTAL                                                        *
 *   NUMBER OF CONTROL CARDS PROCESSED IN THE RUN.  CPU TIME IS THE AMOUNT OF                                                       *
 *   TIME THE CONTROL CARD HAS SPENT IN "ACTUAL" EXECUTION, USING CPU RESOURCES.                                                    *
 *   SRB TIME IS THE AMOUNT OF TIME THE SYSTEM HAS SPENT WHILE PERFORMING SER-                                                      *
 *   VICE REQUESTS, PRIORITY TASKS WHICH AFFECT ANOTHER ADDRESS SPACE.  ELAPSED                                                     *
 *   TIME IS THE TOTAL "WALL CLOCK" TIME THAT THE CONTROL CARDS HAVE SPENT RUN-                                                     *
 *   NING IN THE SYSTEM.  THE TIME FIELDS ARE DISPLAYED AS MINUTES (MM),                                                            *
 *   SECONDS (SS), AND HUNDREDTHS OF SECONDS (HH).                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *   NOTE: OUTPUT FORMAT FOR THE TIME FIELDS IS AS FOLLOWS ==>                                                                      *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *       CPUTIME MM:SS:HH, SRBTIME MM:SS:HH, ELAPSED TIME MM:SS:HH                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                      REQUIRED JCL:                                                                                               *
 *         -------------------------------------------------                                                                        *
 *         NO SPECIAL JCL IS NEEDED FOR THIS FUNCTION                                                                               *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
 *                                                                                                                                  *
